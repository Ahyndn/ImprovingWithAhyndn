## Post-Convocation Scramble

Long after convocation, shortly before 2020, I didn’t know what life without school meant. Was I pleased? Very. Yet, I had to let the years sink in for a moment as I intake the my new reality: I finished as a terrible student and I had a new degree.  

As a student, my best performance, I always felt I was mediocre and at worst, my peers, my educators got really worried for me because when I’m doing bad, I’m not okay. So, I believed that no grad school was no surprise and I accepted it.

I knew early about the critical need of good people relations [5-6] and so, I started my scramble with meeting some nice people, had a good start with trying to find my continuation beyond school; however, all that effort sadly wasn’t enough. My efforts were not enough during the time that occurred after convocation and before the catastrophe of 2020 with my applications for employers.

## Still Seeing Computers from the 2000s

The terrible 2020 health phenomenon had not arrived yet, and I still continued to talk with some nice people, I came realize that I can use this time to self-develop my proficiency with a computer. Of all of the words passed back and forth, I got a few tips that essentially led me to determine that my first four steps:

1. I need to focus on doing research about computers. 
2. Then I could work on expanding my career options.
3. With the complications of 2020 in the background, I definitely had time. 
4. I must hustle for earnings and I must hustle to learn.
